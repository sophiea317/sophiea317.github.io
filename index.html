about me research



I am a first-year PhD student at Yale University in the Department of Psychology, where I am advised by Nick Turk-Browne in the Turk-Browne Lab. I am interested in understanding the dynamic interplay between our past, present, and future experiences. I am particularly interested in exploring the mechanisms through which our current experiences become integrated with our past and how our past experiences influence the encoding of new memories and shape the way we perceive and think about the future.

I received a Bachelor of Science in Psychology and Biomathematics at Florida State University, where I worked with Chris Martin in the Martin Memory Lab. There, I studied how we selectively retrieve semantic and episodic information using fMRI, receiving the Barry Goldwater Scholarship.
